Wave Forces

The floating body on which the wave forces are applied is approximated by a triangulated mesh. The forces are calculated for each triangle of the body and then summed to be applied to its center of mass.

Pressure Drag Forces

The pressure drag forces are trying to capture both the forces allowing a boat to turn, limiting its ability to drift sideways, and the planing forces which push the boat out of the water. These forces act along the normal of the surface.

if cosθicos\theta_icosθi​ positive

Where:

  • fpf_pfp​ : falloff power of the facing dot product. It controls how fast or slow the drag falls off when the surface's normal transitions from parallel to the point velocity, when the drag force should reach its peak intensity, to perpendicular to it when the drag force should vanish.
  • CPD1C_{PD1}CPD1​ : linear drag coefficient
  • CPD2C_{PD2}CPD2​ : quadratic drag coefficient
  • vrv_rvr​ : reference speed
  • n→\overrightarrow{n}n : the normal to the surface

Similarly for the suction drag we have:

Where:

  • fsf_sfs​ : falloff power for suction
  • CSD1C_{SD1}CSD1​ : linear suction drag coefficient
  • CSD2C_{SD2}CSD2​ : quadratic suction drag coefficient

Damping

Forces that are used to dampen the linear and angular movement indepentantly

F→lin=v→(−rs∗cDampL1+cDampL2∗linSpeed)\overrightarrow{F}_{lin} = \overrightarrow{v} (-rs * cDampL1 + cDampL2 * linSpeed) Flin​=v(−rs∗cDampL1+cDampL2∗linSpeed)

Where:

  • v→\overrightarrow{v}v : the linear velocity of the vehicle
  • rsrsrs : the ratio between the submerged area and the total surface area of the model
  • cDampL1,cDampL2cDampL1, cDampL2cDampL1,cDampL2 : linear movement coefficients
  • linSpeedlinSpeedlinSpeed : the linear speed of the vehicle

F→ang=ω→(−rs∗cDampR1+cDampR2∗angSpeed)\overrightarrow{F}_{ang} = \overrightarrow{\omega} (-rs * cDampR1 + cDampR2 * angSpeed) Fang​=ω(−rs∗cDampR1+cDampR2∗angSpeed)

  • ω→\overrightarrow{\omega}ω : the angular velocity of the vehicle
  • rsrsrs : the ratio between the submerged area and the total surface area of the model
  • cDampR1,cDampR2cDampR1, cDampR2cDampR1,cDampR2 : angular movement coefficients
  • angSpeedangSpeedangSpeed : the angular speed of the vehicle

Implementation

double area = this->data->area;
double subArea = this->data->submergedArea;
double rs = subArea / area;

// Force
auto& v = this->data->linVelocity;
double linSpeed = std::sqrt(v.squared_length());
double cL = - rs * (cDampL1 + cDampL2 * linSpeed);
cgal::Vector3 force = v * cL;

//Torque
auto& omega = this->data->angVelocity;
double angSpeed = std::sqrt(omega.squared_length());
double cR = - rs * (cDampR1 + cDampR2 * angSpeed);
cgal::Vector3 torque = omega * cR;

this->data->force  += force;
this->data->torque += torque;

Added Mass

The added mass force is calculated using Newton's second law of motion:

F→=−Camaa→\overrightarrow{F} = -C_a m_a \overrightarrow{a} F=−Ca​ma​a

Where:

  • CaC_aCa​ : the added mass coefficient
  • mam_ama​ : the mass of the displaced fluid
  • aaa : the acceleration of the body

Since the volume of the submerged body is known, the mass of the displaced fluid can be calculated by multiplying this volume with the water density. We only consider the planar motion of the body for the added mass force, so the zzz component of the force is always zero.

Engine Thrust

For the engine thrust after the thrust message is published a PID controller is used to apply wrenches directly to the propeller link. The angular velocity of the blades is calculated by the equations described in Fossen's "Guidance and Control of Ocean Vehicles" in page 246.

ω=TρCTd4\omega = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\rho C_T d^4}} ω=ρCT​d4T​​

Where:

  • TTT : thrust
  • ρ\rhoρ : the density of water
  • CTC_TCT​ : thurst coefficient which relates the angular velocity to actual thrust
  • ddd : propeller diameter
